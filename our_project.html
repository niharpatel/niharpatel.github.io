Our Data

The National Alzhiemer's Coordinating Center is a NIH funded center that aggregates and standardizes dementia questioneers through their various centers. Individuals who administer these tests are specially trained so the data is of a consistent and standard quality. While their have been some updates to this methodology, the data can be standardize and is heavily documented.

The dataset consists of approximately 33 thousands subjects who are assessed across 725 variables. The dataset also contains multiple observation per patient since individuals are tracked throughout their treatment or until they move away.

To constuct our model we standardized our data and separated it into a train, development, and test sets along standard 60/20/20 splits.
